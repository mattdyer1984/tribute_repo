George Best.

The Greatest player of all time?

George Best's life:

  • 1946 - Born in Cregagh, Belfast, Nothern Ireland
  • 1961 - Joins Manchester United as a 15 year old
  • 1963 - Made his Manchester United debut aged 17, against West Bromich Albion in the old First Division
  • 1964 - 2 Months later, in his second first team appearance, he scored his first professional goal against Burnley in a 5-1 win. Best would go on to make 26 appearances for the first team that season, Manchester Utd finished second in the league to Liverpool.

    • George Best pictured with the European Cup 1968.
  • 1965 - In his first full season as a professional Best's 14 goals would help United to the Division One league title. Best also made his debut for Northern Ireland in this season
  • 1966 - In whats regarded as one of his best performances,Best scores twice as United become the first team to inflict a European defeat on a Benfica side in the Stadium of Light. United win 5-1 and Best is hailed as 'El Beatle' by the fans.
  • 1968 - Best plays a pivotol part in Manchester Uniteds season, winning the European Cup.
  • 1970 - Best scores six goals in an FA Cup fifth-round tie at Northampton. However he is sent off whilst playing for Northern Ireland against Scotland.
  • 1972 - Best announces his retirement after a bust up with Man Utds manager Tommy Doherty, he would return for a short while before once agin falling out with the manager.
  • 1973-83 - Best would continue playing for a host of clubs including Fulham, Stockport, Los Angeles Aztecs, Fort Lauderdale Strikers and finally plays briefly for Bournemouth. By this time best had begun treatment for alcoholism which ultimately had led to the decline of his career. Best finished his career having won the Division 1 title twice with Manchester United and the European cup in 1968. Best was also top scorer in Division One in the 1967/68 season and awarded the FWA:European player of the in 1968 as well as the Ballon D'or winner for 1968.

    • Best during his time with LA Aztecs.
  • 1984 Best continued to struggle with his alcohol addiction, recieving a 3 month jail sentence for a drink driving offence.
  • 1996 - Best publicly announces he would be interested in succeeding Jack Charlton as the Northern Irleand manager however the FAI decide to appoint Milwalls then boss Mick Mcarthy
  • 1998 - Best is appointed as a pundit on Sky Sports, featuring the thier Saturday afternoon broadcast Soccer Saturday hosted by Jeff Stelling
  • 2002 - Best's continued acholism ultimately led to liver failure, in 2002 Best was given a liver transplant. He was also awarded a BBC Sports Personality: Life time achievement award.
  • 2004 - Best's continued struggles with alchol lead to another drink driving conviction and further declines in his health.
  • 2005 - Best is admnitted to the private Cromwell Hospital in west London, Doctors reveal Best is in intensive care with kidney problems, despite some initial improvement Best eventually succumbs to his illness and dies on ther 25th November aged 59.

Best was widely reagrded as one of the most talented footballers to have played the game, had it not been for his off field struggles many believe he would now be regarded as one of if not the best player of all time.
